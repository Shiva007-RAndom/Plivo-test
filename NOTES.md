# NOTES

**Signal used:** The model is a per-language `RandomForestClassifier` (100 trees, depth 3) trained on eight causal prosodic features extracted from the ~2.5s of audio immediately before a pause — trailing energy level and decay rate, F0 level and slope over the last voiced stretch, final-syllable lengthening ratio, and speaker-relative normalized versions of the energy and pitch features. It outputs `p_eot`, the probability a pause is a true end-of-turn rather than a hold, which a threshold-plus-timeout policy then uses to decide when to respond. Evaluated with grouped 5-fold cross-validation, it cuts English response delay 30% versus a silence-only timer (1600ms → 1120ms) at the same 5% false-interruption budget, and raises AUC from chance (~0.50) to 0.652 (English) and 0.691 (Hindi).

**Where it still fails:** The classic textbook cue — falling pitch signals sentence end — is nearly a coin flip in this data: pauses with falling pitch were actually holds 52% of the time, and every one of the eight features shows similarly heavy class overlap on inspection. Spectrograms of the worst errors confirm this directly, showing genuine turn-ends with rising pitch and mid-list holds with the "textbook" falling-pitch pattern. Hindi's response delay is tied with the naive baseline not because the model is weak (AUC still climbed 0.19) but because the data's pause-duration distribution sets an ~800ms floor no classifier can beat within the 5% budget. Together this suggests prosody alone is near its ceiling for distinguishing holds from ends, and the residual ambiguity is likely lexical/semantic rather than acoustic.

**With one more day:** Add lexical/semantic signal (e.g. partial ASR transcript, syntactic completeness, or a lightweight language model over the trailing words) since prosody-only features appear to have plateaued around 52% separability. I'd also collect or synthesize more Hindi pauses with longer natural hold durations to test whether the ~850ms floor is a genuine property of the language or an artifact of this particular dataset.
